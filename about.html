A Little Bit About Me

Hello there! My name is Kendall Mangrum and I'm from the small town of Shelbyville, Indiana which is located about 30 mintues to the southeast of Indianapolis. I have lived in Shelby County my entire life, besides when in Bloomington for college, so I am very used to living out in the country and being surrounded by fields of corn and soybeans.

I graduated from the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering at Indiana University Bloomington in the spring of 2021 with a B.S. in Informatics with minors with Computer Science and Public Health. While at IU, I was a member of the inaugural Luddy Living Learning Community, a member of the Hutton Honors College, and an Undergraduate Instructor for an introduction to computers and computing course.

I have had a wide array of interests and hobbies over the years. When I find some free time, I enjoy reading, biking, hiking, playing video games, hanging out with friends, and being with family. Usually some of these things overlap, as I prefer to do things with friends and family rather than by myself. One of my favorite things to do is to just sit around a fire talking the night away with friends.
